Screencasting

Capturing on-screen processes to create instructional content.

I used screencasting to record and narrate on-screen steps, demonstrating how to utilize Canva's AI video editing features while providing clear, visual instructions for learners.

Screencast of Canva's AI Video Editing Features

Using Canva’s AI video editing features gave me a deeper understanding of how artificial intelligence can simplify and enhance the video creation process in educational contexts. The AI tools I used, such as video, voice, music, and text generators, made simplified my editing and made it efficient and untaught. I was able to focus more on designing meaningful visuals and narration rather than spending time on complex technical steps. In educational design, tools like Canva’s can greatly benefit teachers and students by making video production more accessible. They allow users to create instructional or demonstration videos that support visual learning and improve engagement. The platform’s AI assistance encourages creativity and helps educators quickly create learning materials they can share with their students. However, automation also comes with some limitations. The AI sometimes produces generic layouts, images, videos, and audio, limiting originality. Human prompts may be interpreted ineffectively, causing educators to spend more time refining their prompts so AI can generate what they are specifically looking for. Additionally, certain advanced features require a paid subscription, which may restrict access for some users. Overall, exploring Canva’s AI video editing tools showed me how technology can support education by making multimedia creation easier and more effective. Still, human creativity and intentional design remain essential to ensure that the final product feels authentic and purposeful.
